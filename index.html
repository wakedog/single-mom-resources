Your Situation & What's Available

You are a single mother with no income and two infants. Here is the honest picture — you qualify for significant help.

Cash Assistance

Monthly Cash Aid

In California (CalWORKs): a family of 3 (you + 2 infants) with zero income can receive up to $983/month in Region 2 (Butte County) as of Oct 2025.

In Ohio (Ohio Works First): cash benefits for a family of 3, typically $400–$500/month. Ohio's TANF is more restrictive but still available immediately.

Food Assistance

CalFresh / SNAP + WIC

A household of 3 with zero income qualifies for $785/month in SNAP/CalFresh food benefits (federal rate FY2025–26).

WIC provides additional food specifically for infants and mothers: infant formula, cereals, milk, eggs, produce. Both states have WIC offices with same-week appointments.

Healthcare

Medi-Cal / Medicaid — Free

You and both infants qualify for free full health coverage immediately. In CA, CalWORKs automatically enrolls you in Medi-Cal. In OH, Ohio Medicaid covers the whole family. Includes doctor visits, prescriptions, dental, vision, mental health. Infants are a priority — same-day approval often possible.

Housing

Rental Assistance + Shelter

Both areas have emergency family shelters and rental assistance. CA has Housing Authority of Butte County with Section 8 vouchers. OH has Stark Metropolitan Housing Authority. Warning: Section 8 waitlists are long (1–3 years) — apply immediately even while pursuing other options.

💡 Your Counselor Says — The #1 Most Important Thing to Understand

  • Apply for everything at once — benefits don't cancel each other out. CalWORKs/OWF, SNAP, WIC, Medicaid, and Housing Assistance are all meant to be received simultaneously.
  • Your children being infants is actually a significant advantage. Many programs prioritize families with infants under 2. Use this — say it clearly every time you call.
  • Keep a folder (physical or phone) with: birth certificates, your ID, Social Security cards, and any proof of address. You'll need these repeatedly.
  • Apply online OR by phone first, then follow up in person. Don't wait for an in-person appointment to start the clock on your benefits — backdating to your application date is real money.
  • If you are in Chico, CA — use 211 or call Butte County DESS at 1-877-410-8803. If in Alliance, OH — call Stark County JFS at 330-452-4661. These are your two most important phone numbers.

💰 Tax Credits — Money You're Owed (Even With No Income This Year)

Single parents with children qualify for some of the largest refundable tax credits available. Even if you didn't work in 2026, file a return — there are credits that don't require earnings, and back-credits if you didn't file last year.

Refundable Credit

Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

The single largest anti-poverty cash benefit in the US. A single parent with two children can receive up to roughly $7,000 back at tax time, even if you only worked part of the year. The credit is refundable — you get cash back even if you owed no tax.

  • 🌐irs.gov/eitc
  • 💡Even small W-2 earnings or 1099 work qualifies — file even if it was just $1,000
  • 💡You can file up to 3 years back — missed 2023 or 2024? Still possible.
Per-Child Credit

Child Tax Credit (CTC)

Up to $2,000 per child on your federal return. A portion is refundable (the "Additional CTC") meaning you get cash back even with low/no tax owed. For your two infants this can be up to $4,000 in addition to EITC.

  • 🌐irs.gov/ctc
  • 💡Requires children to have Social Security Numbers — get them early
Free DIY

MyFreeTaxes.com — File Online Free

United Way's free online tax filing platform. Designed for low-income filers with simple returns. Walks you through every credit you qualify for. Works on a phone.

  • 🌐myfreetaxes.com
  • ⚡Federal + state, fully free, no income limit for self-prep
  • 💡Helpline if you get stuck: 1-866-698-2040
In-Person Free Help

VITA — Volunteer Income Tax Assistance

Free in-person tax preparation by IRS-certified volunteers. Open seasonally, January through April. Bring photo ID, SSN cards for self and infants, and any income documents. Site finder below.

  • 📞IRS VITA locator: 1-800-906-9887
  • 🌐irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep
  • 📍Chico VITA: see Chico tab · Alliance VITA: see Alliance tab

⏱️ Counselor's Honest Assessment on Wait Times

  • Cash aid (CalWORKs / OWF) is where delays hurt most. Prepare to go 2–4 weeks without it. This is why getting food (SNAP/WIC) fast and food bank access is critical — it reduces cash pressure.
  • SNAP food benefits backdated to your application date means applying online TODAY and getting approved in week 2 still gives you the full month's benefits. Start the clock immediately.
  • Medicaid for infants is the fastest program in both states. If your infants have no insurance right now, this is your Day 1 priority call.
  • Section 8 housing vouchers are a long game — 1 to 3 years is real. Apply today so you're in the queue. Pursue transitional housing (YWCA, Alliance for Children) for the near term.
  • Always follow up after 10 business days if you haven't heard anything. Ask for your caseworker's direct number. Write down every date and who you spoke with.

Counselor's Tips & Insider Guidance

Hard-won practical advice to help you navigate the system faster and avoid common mistakes.

📋 The "One Application Unlocks Everything" Strategy

In both CA and OH, applying for CalWORKs / Ohio Works First at your county office submits you for cash, food (SNAP), and Medicaid in ONE application. Don't make separate trips. One form, one appointment, three programs. Always say "I want to apply for all available assistance."

🗓️ Backdate Strategy — The Clock Matters

Both SNAP and CalWORKs benefits are backdated to the date you applied, not the date you were approved. Apply online or by phone TODAY, even if your appointment isn't for two weeks. This could mean an extra $800–$1,000 in food benefits you won't lose to processing delays.

👶 "Two Infants" Is Your Golden Key

Every time you call an agency, lead with: "I am a single mother with two infants and no income." This is not manipulation — it is accurate information that triggers priority processing in almost every program. Infants with no healthcare, no formula, no heat are a documented emergency in every state system.

📁 Build a "Benefits Binder" on Day 1

Get a $1 folder or accordion file. Keep every paper you get: every Notice of Action, every approval letter, every case number. Write down every phone call with date, time, and name of person. This protects you if benefits are wrongly cut and makes appeals much easier.

🏠 CA-Specific: Housing Authority Drop Box

The Housing Authority of Butte County has a 24/7 drop box at their office. You can submit your housing application any hour, any day, without waiting for office hours. This means you can apply for Section 8 this evening. The timestamp matters for waitlist position.

🏠 OH-Specific: Call the 2-1-1 First in Alliance

Dialing 2-1-1 in Stark County connects you to United Way's Help Central, which has a live database of what's available right now — including emergency food, clothing, and shelter beds. It's free, 24/7, and much faster than calling individual agencies cold.

💊 WIC Formula = Real Money

Infant formula costs $200–$400/month per infant at retail. WIC provides this for free. For two infants, that can be $400–$800/month in formula alone — on top of other WIC foods. This is often MORE valuable than the initial cash aid payment. Get this appointment on the calendar first.

⚡ Expedited SNAP — Ask the Magic Words

When applying for SNAP/CalFresh, say: "I have no income, two infants, and cannot wait 30 days. Do I qualify for expedited processing?" Federal law requires states to process expedited SNAP within 7 days (and often 3). You almost certainly qualify. Workers don't always offer this proactively — you must ask.

🚨 What To Do If Benefits Are Delayed or Wrongly Denied

You have the right to a fair hearing in both states. If your application is denied or delayed past 30 days, request a "fair hearing" or "appeal" in writing. In CA, Legal Services of Northern California (Chico: 541 Normal Ave) provides free help. In OH, call Ohio Legal Help at (844) 894-4529 or contact ICAN (330) 455-9100 in Canton.

👩‍⚕️ Mental Health Support — Use It

Both areas have free mental health services for parents. In Chico, NVCSS offers free counseling (530) 241-0552. In Alliance, CommQuest provides mental health services at 1207 West State St, Suite M, (330) 821-8407. Raising two infants alone under financial stress is incredibly hard. This resource exists for exactly your situation.

  • 💡Saying "all available assistance" opens every program in one application

Asking for expedited food benefits

"I have no income and less than $100 in my bank account. I have two infants, one under 1 year old and a 2 year old, who need to eat. Do I qualify for expedited SNAP processing? Federal law requires 7 days for expedited cases."

  • ⚡Workers don't always offer expedited proactively — you must ask
  • ⚡This drops SNAP approval from 30 days to 3–7 days

Getting Medicaid for the infants TODAY

"I'm a single mother of two infants — one under 1 year old and a 2 year old — and they have no health insurance. I need to enroll them in Medi-Cal/Medicaid today. Children under 1 are a priority — can you enroll them same-day?"

  • ⚡Get the case number / member ID before you hang up
Script 4 — Shelter Tonight

Calling the shelter hotline

"I'm calling because I need shelter tonight. I'm a single mother with two infants — one under 1 year old and a 2 year old. I have no safe place to stay. What family shelters have openings or what's the assessment process?"

🚧 When Things Go Wrong — Troubleshooting

Real problems you may face — and exactly what to do.

Denial

"My application was denied"

You have 90 days to request a Fair Hearing (CA) or State Hearing (OH) — file it in writing. Even if you think the denial is correct, request the hearing — many denials get overturned. Get the denial letter, write "I request a fair hearing on [date of denial letter]" + your name and case number, and mail/email/hand-deliver it. Legal aid (LSNC in Chico, Community Legal Aid in Canton) will help you for free.

Delay

"It's been 30+ days, no answer"

SNAP must be decided within 30 days (7 days expedited). CalWORKs/OWF within 45 days. Past those deadlines, you have a right to your benefits. Call your caseworker. If they don't respond within 48 hours, ask for a supervisor. Still nothing? File a fair hearing request — that always triggers movement. Document every call with date/time/name.

Lost / Stolen

"My EBT card was lost or stolen"

Call 1-877-328-9677 (EBT customer service, both states) IMMEDIATELY to freeze and replace it. Replacement takes 5–7 business days. Federal law now requires states to replace stolen EBT benefits — ask your county worker to file the theft claim. Report the theft to police for the report number.

Missed Appointment

"I missed my CalWORKs / OWF appointment"

Call your caseworker that same day and ask to reschedule. Most counties allow one missed appointment if you call. If your benefits were already cut, you have 90 days to request a fair hearing — and benefits can be restored to the date they were stopped if you reapply within 30 days.

Wrong Amount

"My benefits dropped without warning"

You should have received a Notice of Action (NOA) at least 10 days before any reduction. If you didn't get one, the cut is procedurally improper. Request a fair hearing within 10 days for "aid paid pending" — your old amount continues during the appeal. If you got the NOA but disagree, you still have 90 days to appeal.

Eviction

"I got a 3-day or eviction notice"

Don't ignore it. Call legal aid immediately (LSNC for CA, Community Legal Aid for OH). They handle eviction defense for free. You may qualify for emergency rental assistance (CalWORKs Homeless Assistance in CA, PRC funds in OH) — ask your benefits caseworker. Apply to the shelter line same day as a backup.

Caseworker Issue

"My caseworker is unreachable / rude"

Ask for the supervisor by name. If unsatisfied, file a written complaint with the county welfare director. In CA, you can also call the state ombudsman: 1-866-613-3777. In OH, contact Ohio Department of Job and Family Services Family Stability Unit: 1-866-244-0071. Always document who, what, when.

No ID

"I have no ID, no birth cert, no SSN card"

You can still apply — agencies cannot deny benefits just for missing documents. They'll give you a "verification needed" letter. To get the documents free: SSA (800) 772-1213 for SSN cards · State Vital Records for birth certs (CA: 916-445-2684 / OH: 614-466-2531) · DMV/BMV with fee waiver if on benefits. LSNC and Community Legal Aid can help if there's a complication.

📞 Your Two Most Important Phone Numbers

  • If you're in Chico, CA: Butte County DESS — 1-877-410-8803. This one number connects you to CalWORKs, CalFresh, Medi-Cal, and housing assistance.
  • If you're in Alliance, OH: Stark County JFS — (330) 452-4661 or statewide 1-844-640-6446. This connects you to OWF/TANF, SNAP, Medicaid, and childcare.
  • Both locations: Dial 2-1-1 at any time, 24 hours a day. This is the fastest way to find what's open right now, today.
